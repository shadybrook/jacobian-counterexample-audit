# Limitations and non-claims

Status date: 21 July 2026.

1. This repository is an independent audit and working note, not a refereed
   article and not an arXiv submission.
2. It does not claim discovery of the displayed polynomial map. The primary
   public attribution is Levent Alpoge's announcement, which credits Akhil for
   the question and Fable for work on the example.
3. It does not claim priority for the inverse cubic, generic degree, monodromy,
   completion, or related-conjecture consequences. Same-day public sources
   overlap materially with several of these observations.
4. The exact symbolic scripts certify polynomial identities. They do not by
   themselves certify literature priority, theorem hypotheses quoted from
   other papers, or sociological acceptance.
5. The two-dimensional Jacobian problem is not settled by this map. Stable
   degree reductions increase dimension.
6. No explicit optimized cubic-homogeneous or Druzkowski counterexample is
   produced here; only existence via published reduction theorems is recorded.
7. The general weighted-lift family is summarized and finite instances are
   regression-tested, but a full classification of that construction is not
   claimed.
8. A restricted "minimal PDE ansatz" claim from the motivating conversation is
   deliberately excluded: the class and equivalence relation were not stated
   precisely enough to support a publication-grade minimality theorem.
9. The current-status search is time-stamped. Later papers can supersede the
   priority and status discussion.
10. The equivariant quotient square law applies to the displayed weight
    pattern; it is not a theorem about arbitrary group quotients or every
    possible descent to dimension two.
11. The finite-field formulas are proved by root-subset counting and checked
    computationally for small prime fields. They do not by themselves provide
    a new characteristic-zero counterexample or settle arithmetic questions
    about all higher-degree lift families.
